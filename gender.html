Male: A person who identifies as a man.

Female: A person who identifies as a woman.

Non-binary: A person who doesn't identify strictly as male or female.

Genderqueer: A non-conforming gender identity, often rejecting the traditional gender binary.

Genderfluid: A person whose gender identity shifts over time.

Agender: A person who doesn't identify with any gender.

Bigender: A person who identifies with two genders, either simultaneously or alternately.

Two-Spirit: A term used by some Indigenous cultures to describe a person who embodies both masculine and feminine spirits.

Demiboy: A person who partially identifies as male.

Demigirl: A person who partially identifies as female.

Pangender: A person who identifies with many or all genders.

Transgender Male: A person assigned female at birth, but identifies as male.

Transgender Female: A person assigned male at birth, but identifies as female.

Androgynous: A person who embodies both masculine and feminine characteristics.

Cisgender: A person whose gender identity matches the sex they were assigned at birth.

Neutrois: A person who feels neutral or lacks gender.

Intergender: A person who identifies as being in between male and female, or a mix of both.

Kathoey: A term in Thailand often used for transgender women or effeminate men.

Hijra: A traditional third gender in South Asia, often recognized as neither male nor female.

Fa'afafine: A gender identity in Samoa, often referring to someone who is raised as a male but embodies female gender roles.

Muxe: A gender identity in Zapotec cultures in Mexico, often assigned male at birth but adopting feminine gender roles.

Autigender: A gender identity that is specifically tied to the experience of being on the autism spectrum.

Graygender: A person whose gender identity feels faint or unclear.

Gendervoid: A gender experience that feels like an absence or lack of gender.

Polygender: A person who identifies with multiple genders, either simultaneously or at different times.

Third-gender: A person who identifies as a gender outside the traditional male and female categories.

Aliagender: A gender identity that doesn't fit within any of the known gender categories.

Quoigender: A gender experience defined by confusion or uncertainty.

Demiflux: A gender identity that shifts between some genders, but remains fixed in certain ways.
